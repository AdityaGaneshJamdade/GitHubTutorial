Free Fire is a free-to-play battle royale game developed and published by Garena, first released in December 2017 for Android and iOS.

In each match, up to 52 players parachute onto an island, search for weapons and supplies, and fight to survive while the safe zone shrinks, forcing intense encounters. The goal is simple: be the last player or team standing.

Known for its fast-paced 10-minute matches, diverse roster of characters with unique abilities, and signature features like the “Gloo Wall” for instant cover, Free Fire quickly became one of the most downloaded mobile games worldwide.

By 2019 it was the most downloaded game globally, and by 2021 it had over 150 million daily active users. Garena later launched Free Fire MAX, an enhanced version with improved graphics and effects.

The game’s esports scene, especially the Free Fire World Series, set records with millions of live viewers. Today, Free Fire remains a cultural phenomenon, blending accessible gameplay with competitive depth and a vibrant community.
